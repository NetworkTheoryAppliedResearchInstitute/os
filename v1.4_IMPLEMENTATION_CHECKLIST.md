## 💰 Budget Tracking

### Hardware Testing Budget

**Option 1: Purchase Used** ($3,000-5,000)
- $150-250 per computer x 20 = $3,000-5,000
- Pros: Full control, can test anytime
- Cons: Upfront cost

**Option 2: Borrow** ($0)
- From friends, family, schools, offices
- Pros: Free
- Cons: Limited time, must return

**Option 3: Hybrid** ($1,000-2,000)
- Buy 10 key computers
- Borrow 10 others
- Pros: Balance of cost and control

**Recommendation**: Hybrid approach

### Where to Acquire Computers

**Purchase**:
- eBay (used business computers)
- Facebook Marketplace
- Local computer recyclers
- School/university surplus sales

**Borrow**:
- Friends/family with old computers
- Local schools (often have old lab computers)
- Offices upgrading hardware
- Community repair cafes

## 🚨 Risk Management

### Risk: Can't acquire 20 computers

**Mitigation**:
- Start with 10 computers (most common brands)
- Crowdsource additional testing
- Accept that 95% coverage is acceptable (not 100%)

### Risk: BIOS photos incomplete

**Mitigation**:
- Focus on top 5 brands (Dell, HP, Lenovo, ASUS, Acer)
- Accept community submissions
- Update guide incrementally (web-based)

### Risk: Compatibility checker inaccurate

**Mitigation**:
- Test on known hardware first
- Iterate based on real results
- Mark as "beta" initially
- Improve accuracy over time (90% → 95% → 99%)
