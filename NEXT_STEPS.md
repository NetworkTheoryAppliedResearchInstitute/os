## 🤔 Questions You Might Have

### Q: Is the timeline realistic now?

**A**: Yes. The adjusted timeline (28 months) is based on:
- CompTIA industry standards
- Realistic effort estimates for complex features
- Buffer time for testing and iteration
- Parallel work streams where possible

Previous timeline (24 months) was **achievable but rushed**. New timeline (28 months) is **achievable comfortably**.

---

### Q: Why defer iOS to v1.1?

**A**: Three reasons:
1. **Time**: iOS app needs 10+ weeks, was budgeted 3 weeks
2. **Value**: iOS background limitations reduce storage contribution value
3. **Focus**: One excellent app (Android) better than two mediocre ones

iOS can be added in v1.1 (3-4 months) after learning from Android launch.

---

### Q: What if I don't want to spend $3K-5K on test computers?

**A**: Three alternatives:
1. **Borrow** from friends, family, schools, offices (free)
2. **Hybrid**: Buy 5 key computers ($750-1,250), borrow rest
3. **Crowdsource**: Recruit beta testers to test on their hardware

**Recommendation**: Hybrid approach (buy 5 most common, borrow 15 others)

---

### Q: Can we start Phase 1 before finalizing v1.4 spec?

**A**: Yes! You can start hardware testing (Milestone 1.5) in parallel with spec finalization. The new sections document what you'll build, but don't block starting work.

**Start immediately**:
- Hardware acquisition

### Q: What's the most important change in v1.4?

**A**: **Hardware compatibility documentation**. This is the #1 reason installations fail. Users need to know:
- Will NTARI work on my computer?
- What WiFi chipset do I have?
- How do I access BIOS?
- What if installation fails?

v1.4 answers all these questions comprehensively.
